Bio:

Marshall Bruce Mathers III, known professionally as Eminem, is an American rapper and music producer. He is credited with popularizing hip hop in Middle America and is regarded as among the greatest rappers of all time. His success is considered to have broken racial barriers to the acceptance of white rappers in popular music. While much of his transgressive work during the late 1990s and early 2000s made him a controversial figure, he came to be a representation of popular angst of the American underclass and has been cited as influencing many musical artists.

Family:

Eminem, born Marshall Bruce Mathers III, is the only child of Marshall Bruce Mathers, Jr. and Deborah R. “Debbie” Nelson. He has been married to Kimberly Anne Scott, with whom he has a daughter named Hailie Jade. Eminem is also related to Alaina Scott, his adopted daughter.

Fun Facts:

  • Eminem holds the Guinness World Record for the most words in a hit single.
  • Eminem has won 15 Grammy Awards, including Best Rap Album four times.
  • His album "The Marshall Mathers LP" is one of the fastest-selling albums in history.

Timeline:

  • 1972: Born in St. Joseph MO.
  • 1988: Started rapping as MC Double M.
  • 1996: Released debut album "Infinite".
  • 2000: Eminem's "The Marshall Mathers LP" became a worldwide success, Grammy-nominated.
  • 2002: Played himself in "8 Mile", won Academy Award for "Lose Yourself".
  • 2004: Went on hiatus due to prescription drug addiction.
  • 2010: "Recovery" was the best-selling album worldwide of the year.
  • 2013: "The Marshall Mathers LP 2" reached US number-one album.
  • 2017: Inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.
  • 2020: "Music To Be Murdered By" became his tenth consecutive US number-one album.
  • 2021: Opened Mom's Spaghetti restaurant in Detroit, MI.
  • 2024: Announced new album "The Death of Slim Shady (Coup de Grâce)".
